Let's practice English grammar through Polish-to-English translation.

# Prompt 1

Give me one Polish sentence at a time that tests a specific grammar 
point. Don't give me the next sentence until I've answered the 
current one.

Cover these topics, cycling through them (tell me which topic each 
sentence belongs to before I answer, or let me guess if I ask):

1. Conditional sentences — zero, first, second, third, mixed
2. Reported speech — tense shifts, plus exceptions and reporting 
   verbs like suggest, insist, deny
3. Passive voice — all tenses, plus "have something done" 
   (e.g. "I had my car repaired")
4. Past modals — must have, might have, should have, could have 
   (speculation and regret)
5. Inversion for emphasis — "Never have I seen...", "Not only did 
   she..., but also...", "Little did he know..."
6. Cleft sentences — "It was John who...", "What I really need is..."
7. Reduced relative clauses — e.g. "The man standing there" instead 
   of "who is standing there"
8. Wish / if only — as an extension of conditionals

After I translate a sentence to English, check my answer and reply with:
1. My answer (as I wrote it)
2. The correct English version
3. A short explanation of the grammar structure being tested (what 
   it is, why it's used here) and why my answer was right or wrong, 
   including any specific word/vocabulary mistakes
4. My running score (correct/total)

Keep sentences varied in difficulty. If I ask to switch topics or 
focus on just one, do that. If I ask what to study next, suggest 
which of the above areas I'm weakest in based on my answers so far.

# Prompt 2

Wrap up all the sentences from this practice session into a Word 
document. For each sentence, include:

1. Polish (the original sentence)
2. My version (what I translated)
3. Proper version (the correct English translation)
4. Concise grammar structure (one line naming the structure/pattern)
5. Short explanation (why my version was right or wrong, including 
   any vocabulary issues)

Number each sentence, include my final score at the top of the 
document, and give me the file to download.
